## Particle System

### Particle Colors
- `#d4f090` — soft mint sparkles (opacity 0.5–0.65)
- `#c8e878` — chartreuse (opacity 0.4–0.7)
- `#7aaa3a` — vivid green orbs (opacity 0.2–0.4)
- `#5aaa28` — deep green (opacity 0.2–0.4)

### Particle Types
- Small dots: `2–3px`, border-radius `50%`
- Medium orbs: `8–12px`, border-radius `50%`, subtle border
- No floating ingredient objects — pure color particles only

### GSAP Float Animation
```typescript
gsap.to(".cocktail-1933-particle", {
  y: -16,
  x: "random(-8, 8)",
  duration: "random(3, 5)",
  ease: "sine.inOut",
  yoyo: true,
  repeat: -1,
  stagger: { amount: 2.5, from: "random" }
});
```
Note: slow and steady — matches the herbal, considered character of the drink.

## Typography Note
The name "1933" uses slightly wider letter-spacing than other cocktail names:
```css
letter-spacing: 0.08em;
```
This gives it a vintage, archival feeling — like a date stamped on a classified recipe card.

## Mood & Energy
**1933** feels: crisp, cool, considered, herbal.
The gradient moves from deep dark forest edges to a vivid burst of chartreuse and lime at the center — like sunlight filtering through a basil garden on a cool morning. Clean, botanical, surprisingly complex. The name evokes another era — a secret recipe rediscovered.
